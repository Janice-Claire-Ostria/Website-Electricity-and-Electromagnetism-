Magnetic Forces

Magnetic forces are the forces of attraction or repulsion that arise between electrically charged particles due to their motion. These forces are described by the Lorentz force law.

Examples of Magnetic Forces in Action

Magnetic forces are at work in many everyday devices, such as electric motors, generators, and magnetic levitation trains.

Mathematical Representation of Magnetic Forces

The Lorentz force law states that the force on a charged particle is equal to the charge times the sum of the electric field and the cross product of the velocity and the magnetic field:
