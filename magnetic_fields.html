Magnetic Fields

Magnetic fields are invisible fields that exert a force on substances that are sensitive to magnetism. They are created by moving electric charges and magnetic dipoles, and they can be visualized using magnetic field lines.

Visual Representations of Magnetic Fields

Magnetic field lines are a useful way to represent the direction and strength of a magnetic field. The lines emerge from the north pole of a magnet and enter the south pole.

Applications of Magnetic Fields

Magnetic fields have numerous applications in technology, including in electric motors, transformers, and magnetic storage devices.
